How to share files on SharePoint(OneDrive)

Share files and folders directly from your SharePoint(OneDrive). Yes. It's easier than emailing the attachment and pretending it's 2008.

🔗 awiadmin-my.sharepoint.com/my
1

Open your SharePoint(OneDrive)

Click the link above or navigate to your SharePoint(OneDrive) directly. Sign in with your DAG Industries work account if prompted. You'll land on your personal file library.

Quick access: You can also reach SharePoint(OneDrive) from the Microsoft 365 app launcher (the waffle icon ⊞ at the top left of any Microsoft page) → select SharePoint(OneDrive).

2

Find the file or folder you want to share

Browse your files or use the search bar at the top. You can share a single file or an entire folder — SharePoint(OneDrive) is not picky.

Right-click the file → select "Share"

5

Type your colleague's name or email address

In the sharing panel, type your colleague's name or email in the search box. SharePoint will suggest matching people from your organisation — please pick the right one. (We're speaking from experience.)

6

Choose their permission level

Before you hit send, set what your colleague can actually do with this file:

recommended

Can view

They can read and download it. Cannot make changes. Great for most cases.

Can edit

They can open, change, and save directly to your file. Use this only if they genuinely need to.

Think twice before selecting "Can edit." Changes they make will affect your original file. You can always upgrade their access later — you cannot un-edit a file.

7

Add a message (optional) and click "Send"

You can add a short note for context — helpful if they have no idea what they're about to receive — then hit Send. They'll get an email with a direct link to the file.

5

Click "Copy link" from the sharing panel

In the sharing panel, look for the Copy link option at the bottom. Alternatively, right-click the file and select Copy link directly — skips the panel entirely.

6

Check that the link is set to "People in DAG Industries"

A link settings box will appear. Before you copy anything, check what it says. Here's what the options actually mean:

✅
People in DAG Industries
Only colleagues with a DAG work account can open it. Use this by default — every single time.
🔒
People with existing access
Only people you've already shared with can use this link. Good for resending to someone who's lost theirs.
⚠️
Anyone with the link
Literally anyone on the internet. Your cat, your boss's boss, that person who keeps reply-alling the whole company. Avoid this for work files.

If it says "Anyone with the link", click the setting and change it to "People in DAG Industries" before copying. This matters.

7

Click "Copy" — then paste the link wherever you need it

Hit Copy and paste the link into Teams, WhatsApp, an email, a sticky note — wherever your colleague will actually see it. The link is now live.

Heads up: The link doesn't expire automatically. If you need to stop access later, right-click the file → Manage access → remove the link. Which brings us to step 8...

8

Manage or remove access anytime

To see who has access or to remove someone, right-click the file → Manage access. From there you can update permissions or revoke access entirely.

Good habit: Review your shared files regularly — especially when a colleague leaves the company or a project wraps up. Files don't expire on their own, and neither does awkward access.
